Taste Haarlem: 15 Dutch Foods You Can't Miss

If you're a traveler, a newcomer, or a student in Haarlem, Dutch cuisine has some unique – and sometimes surprising – flavors waiting for you. From hearty winter stews to crispy fried snacks, here's a list of 15 Dutch dishes you definitely need to try!

Exploring Haarlem Through Food

As a food lover myself, I created this list with great care. Some dishes might not be in the top rankings of the Dutch cuisine but they are still worth a try. My favorite Dutch food is pannenkoeken. I also love patat met mayo — perfect for warming up on a cold day. Also loving a dropje! What are your favorites?

Top 15 Food in Haarlem

1. Pannenkoeken

Traditional Dutch Dish / Sweet or Savory

Dutch pancakes — thinner than American ones, but bigger and often filled with bacon, cheese, or fruit.

2. Poffertjes

Street Food Favorite / Sweet Treat

Tiny fluffy pancakes, usually served with powdered sugar and butter — a sweet street-food favorite.

3. Stroopwafels

Cafe Classic / Perfect with Coffee

A thin waffle with caramel syrup inside. Best enjoyed warm, placed on top of a hot coffee or tea.

4. Patat met Mayo

Street Snack / Served in Paper Cone

Thick Dutch fries, usually served in a paper cone with creamy mayonnaise (or satay sauce).

5. Bitterballen

Bar Snack / Best with a Cold Beer

Crispy, deep-fried balls filled with savory ragout — the perfect snack with a Dutch beer.

6. Stamppot

Winter Comfort Food / Home-Style Meal

Mashed potatoes mixed with vegetables like kale or sauerkraut, usually served with smoked sausage. Comfort food at its best!

7. Kibbeling

Seafood Snack / Try at the Market

Deep-fried battered fish chunks, served with garlic or tartar sauce. Crunchy outside, soft inside.

8. Kroket

Dutch Fast Food / Crispy & Creamy Inside

Crispy, deep-fried croquettes with a soft ragout filling — a classic Dutch snack found everywhere!

9. Haring

Traditional Snack / Eaten with Onions

Raw herring fish, often eaten with onions and pickles. A true Dutch tradition, especially in Haarlem's markets.

10. Appeltaart

Bakery Favorite / Warm & Cinnamon-Rich

Classic Dutch apple pie, with a rich filling of apples, cinnamon, and raisins. Perfect with whipped cream.

11. Hageslag

Breakfast Tradition / Chocolate on Bread

Chocolate sprinkles eaten on buttered bread — yes, this is a real Dutch breakfast.

12. Dropjes

Dutch Candy / Salty or Sweet Licorice

Dutch people love salty and sweet licorice. It might be unusual for visitors, but worth a try!

13. Suikerwafel

Regional Treat / Crunchy & Caramelized

Sweet sugar waffles from the Brabant region — crunchy and caramelized.

14. Gevulde Koek

Bakery Classic / Almond Paste Inside

Cookies with almond paste inside are a classic Dutch sweet treat popular in bakeries in Haarlem.

15. Speculaas

Holiday Cookie / Spiced with Cinnamon

Spiced cookies, often eaten during the holidays. Full of cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves.
